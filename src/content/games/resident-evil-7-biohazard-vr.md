The first time Jack Baker came at me with a shovel, I didn't flinch because the controller rumbled. I flinched because my actual head told my body that a shovel was about to take off my actual jaw.

That's Resident Evil 7: Biohazard in VR. It doesn't ask you to watch a horror movie. It puts your senses in a decaying Louisiana house and dares you to keep the headset on.

## What This VR Option Actually Is

Resident Evil 7 launched in 2017 with two lives. On PS4, Capcom shipped an official full-campaign VR mode that let you play the entire game through a PSVR headset using a DualShock 4. It was one of the first times a major publisher promised a complete AAA campaign in VR and actually delivered it.

On PC, there was nothing official for years. The PSVR version was locked to console, and PC owners could only look at the Baker plantation through a flat screen. That changed when praydog released REFramework, an engine-level modding platform for Capcom's RE Engine games. The initial RE7 release added generic 6DOF VR with head tracking but no hand controls. By mid-2022, REFramework added full motion controls, roomscale movement, physical weapon aiming, blocking, melee, and over-the-shoulder healing gestures. That is the version worth talking about now.

This article focuses on the PCVR REFramework route, because it is the most complete, best-looking, and most physically involving way to play the game in VR. The PSVR route still matters as a historical touchstone — it proved a full horror campaign could work in a headset — but it is gamepad-only and tied to a last-generation console ecosystem.

## Getting In

On PSVR, you select VR mode from the menu and you're done. On PCVR, you're installing a mod.

REFramework installs by dropping files into the game directory. You choose between an OpenVR build or an OpenXR build, and you may need to switch to the non-ray-traced DirectX 11 build on Steam for the smoothest results. Some players need a small mod to fix ugly crosshatched shadows. Some need to disable HDR. Some need to unplug their gamepad because the mod can get confused if a controller is still connected.

None of this is rocket science, but it is not a one-click installer either. Budget time for a troubleshooting pass. The payoff is a PCVR version that looks sharper than PSVR ever did, with full hand presence and roomscale movement.

## How It Actually Plays

The core game is unchanged. You are Ethan Winters, walking into a nightmare to find your missing wife. The house is claustrophobic, the ammo is scarce, and the Baker family is relentless.

What VR adds is physical stakes. Peeking around a corner means physically leaning. Hearing Jack whisper from behind you means actually turning your head. Jump scares do not just startle — they make you recoil in your play space. After long sessions my shoulders were sore from tensing.

On PCVR with motion controls, aiming a pistol by pointing your dominant hand feels right in a way that head-aiming never fully did. Melee and blocking are motion-driven. Healing requires reaching over your shoulder. The body IK lets you look down and see a torso, which is mostly cool until it clips into your chin — at which point you can disable it in the menu.

The controls are not perfect. Weapon switching uses a quick-select dial rather than physical holsters. There is no manual reloading. The "21" mini-game does not have a draw button mapped for motion controllers, so you may need a keyboard or gamepad for that corner of the DLC. And a connected gamepad can conflict with the VR input, so unplug it.

On PSVR, the experience is more limited but still effective. You use a DualShock 4 for everything, with head movement fine-tuning your aim. The comfort options are generous — adjustable rotation speed, FOV dimming, movement speed tweaks — but the visual fidelity takes a hit, and smooth camera rotation can still feel lurchy.

## Comfort and Performance

This is an intense VR experience by design, not by accident.

The first-person perspective, slow deliberate movement, and claustrophobic environments already put pressure on your vestibular system. Forced camera moves during cutscenes and ladder climbs can be nauseating, especially on PCVR where the mod does not always cushion them. The "Decoupled Camera Pitch" option helps, but it cannot fully fix the fact that the game sometimes grabs the camera and moves it without your permission.

Motion sickness varies wildly by player. If you do not have solid VR legs, expect short sessions and frequent breaks.

Performance on PCVR is demanding. Resident Evil 7 was a good-looking game for 2017, and rendering it twice at high resolution while the REFramework mod injects VR overhead is not cheap. A mid-range PC may need reprojection or lowered resolution to stay smooth. A high-end rig is the realistic target for native 90 fps at a crisp image. The ray-tracing build of the game is particularly punishing; the older non-RT build is generally the safer choice for VR.

## What Works and What Doesn't

The atmosphere is the star. The Baker house is one of the most convincingly disgusting spaces in modern horror, and VR makes every floorboard, cockroach, and bloodstain feel adjacent to your body. The sound design is exceptional — you will physically turn toward noises.

The combat tension translates beautifully. With limited ammo and enemies that can kill you in a few hits, every encounter matters. In VR, that pressure lives in your shoulders.

The weaknesses are mostly implementation friction. Cutscene camera behavior breaks presence. Some boss fights have reported hitbox weirdness on the PCVR mod, requiring a fallback to flatscreen or the older DX11 build. The main menu on PCVR is tiny and awkward. Visual polish is inconsistent — the PCVR version can look great, but only after you chase down the right settings and fixes.

## The Verdict

Resident Evil 7 is one of the best survival horror games of the last decade, and VR makes it measurably better at being scary. The PCVR mod finally gives the PC version the hand-tracked, roomscale body that the PSVR version always lacked. It is not as polished as a native VR game, and it will make you uncomfortable in ways that have nothing to do with frame rates.

If you love horror and you have the hardware and the patience, this is essential. If you get motion sick easily or want a frictionless install, play it flat — the game is still excellent on a monitor. But if you've ever wanted to feel genuinely hunted inside a headset, Resident Evil 7 in VR is one of the strongest arguments for owning one.
