# Review Methodology v1

**Owner:** Richard  
**Date:** 2026-03-14  
**Purpose:** Define how the site evaluates flat games with VR routes consistently, honestly, and in a way readers can trust.

---

## 1. Methodology Goal

The site is not reviewing only the underlying game.
It is reviewing the **quality and viability of that game's VR route**.

That distinction matters.

A brilliant flat game can have a mediocre VR implementation.
A mediocre flat game can become surprisingly compelling in VR.
A fascinating framework experiment can still be a bad recommendation.

The job is to evaluate the **reader experience of playing this title in VR now**.

---

## 2. What We Are Judging

Each review should judge three overlapping things:

1. **Underlying game suitability for VR**
   - Does the game conceptually benefit from VR?

2. **Implementation quality**
   - How well does the VR route actually work?

3. **Practical recommendation value**
   - Is the result worth the setup friction, compromise, and instability?

That means our recommendation is not a generic game score.
It is a decision judgment.

---

## 3. Evidence Standard

### 3.1 Evidence Types
We accept three tiers of evidence:

**Direct Testing [Tested]** — hands-on verification with headset and hardware. Gold standard.

**Research Compilation [Verified]** — cross-referencing multiple independent sources to establish consensus or identify contradictions. Explicitly label sources: [Steam Reviews], [Documentation], [Community Reports], [Video Evidence].

**Single-Source Claims [Hearsay]** — unverified reports from one source. Must be labeled clearly and treated as provisional.

### 3.2 AI Writers: Be Explicit About Limits
AI writers cannot strap on headsets. They must:
- Never fabricate hands-on impressions
- Always label evidence by source type
- Cross-reference conflicting claims rather than cherry-picking
- Flag when direct testing would be needed to resolve uncertainty

The reader should always know what was tested vs compiled.

### 3.3 No false precision
If evidence scope is limited, do not overstate certainty.

### 3.4 No hype inflation
A promising experiment is not automatically a recommendation.

### 3.5 Update when warranted
Pages should be refreshed when new evidence, updates, or testing becomes available.

---

## 4. Evidence Protocol

### For Direct Testing [Tested]
Document:
- headset used
- runtime used
- CPU/GPU/RAM if relevant
- input method
- game version/build
- mod/framework version
- approximate test duration
- what content was tested

### For Research Compilation [Verified]
Document:
- sources consulted (Steam reviews, Reddit, YouTube, Discord, official docs)
- date range of sources
- number of independent reports for key claims
- conflicting claims found and how resolved
- confidence level for each major assertion

### Minimum viable evidence expectation

**Full Review:** Requires either direct testing OR substantial multi-source research with clear methodology disclosure. Single-source claims must be flagged.

**Compatibility / Verdict Page:** Can rely on research compilation if sources are documented and contradictions are addressed.

**When Evidence is Insufficient:** The piece should be labeled as "Preliminary Assessment" or held until better evidence is available.

---

## 5. Scoring Philosophy

Scores are tools for consistency, not substitutes for judgment.

We should use a fixed rubric internally and optionally expose either:
- a summarized final score, or
- only recommendation labels + sub-scores

A single number alone is too blunt. Readers need the shape of the experience.

---

## 6. Core Scoring Categories

Use a 1-5 score for each category.

### 6.1 Setup Friction
How difficult is it to get running and keep running?

**5** = basically frictionless  
**4** = straightforward with mild caveats  
**3** = manageable but requires attention  
**2** = annoying, fragile, or technically demanding  
**1** = painful, brittle, or unreasonable for most users

Judge based on:
- install complexity
- dependency burden
- break risk after updates
- amount of tinkering required

### 6.2 VR Implementation Quality
How convincingly does the game function as a VR experience?

**5** = excellent VR adaptation with few obvious compromises  
**4** = strong implementation with some limitations  
**3** = mixed but meaningfully VR-playable  
**2** = partial/awkward implementation  
**1** = barely functions as credible VR

Judge based on:
- head tracking
- stereoscopy/world scale
- camera behavior
- hand presence/motion controls
- interaction fidelity
- UI/cutscene handling

### 6.3 Playability / Completeness
Can users actually play the game this way in a meaningful sense?

**5** = fully playable end-to-end with minimal caveats  
**4** = mostly playable with occasional issues  
**3** = playable but significant compromises or broken edges  
**2** = limited or inconsistent progress/playability  
**1** = more demo than usable game route

Judge based on:
- campaign viability
- mode support
- progression blockers
- reliability across sessions

### 6.4 Controls / Input Quality
How well do controls map to VR expectations?

**5** = intuitive and strong  
**4** = good with minor awkwardness  
**3** = workable but imperfect  
**2** = clumsy or inconsistent  
**1** = poor enough to undermine the whole route

Judge based on:
- motion controller support
- aiming/melee/interactions
- gamepad fallback quality
- menu/input consistency

### 6.5 Comfort
How tolerable is the experience for a typical VR user?

Important: this score is not "lower is more intense therefore better."  
It measures whether the experience is reasonably manageable and well-handled.

**5** = comfort-conscious, well-managed, broadly tolerable  
**4** = moderate intensity but well-contained  
**3** = clearly intense or uneven but manageable for experienced users  
**2** = regularly uncomfortable  
**1** = likely to cause discomfort for many users due to poor handling

Judge based on:
- locomotion choices
- forced camera movement
- cutscene handling
- shake/vehicle issues
- general comfort hygiene

### 6.6 Performance Efficiency
How demanding is the experience relative to what it delivers?

**5** = efficient and stable on realistic hardware  
**4** = reasonably demanding but predictable  
**3** = moderate issues or substantial tuning required  
**2** = heavy or inconsistent  
**1** = severe performance problems or impractical demand

Judge based on:
- framerate consistency
- heavy scenes/bottlenecks
- stability under load
- required compromises

### 6.7 Stability / Reliability
How likely is the experience to break, crash, or misbehave?

**5** = stable and dependable  
**4** = mostly stable with occasional issues  
**3** = mixed reliability  
**2** = frequent issues or fragility  
**1** = unstable enough to poison the recommendation

Judge based on:
- crash frequency
- visual corruption
- save/load issues
- breakage after patches
- reproducibility of major bugs

### 6.8 Recommendation Strength
This is an editorial synthesis score, not a mechanical average.

**5** = easy recommendation  
**4** = recommended with moderate caveats  
**3** = conditional / specific-audience recommendation  
**2** = hard to recommend broadly  
**1** = not recommended

This score reflects the whole package.

---

## 7. Suggested Weighting

If a composite internal score is used, weight categories as follows:

- Setup Friction — 10%
- VR Implementation Quality — 20%
- Playability / Completeness — 20%
- Controls / Input Quality — 15%
- Comfort — 10%
- Performance Efficiency — 10%
- Stability / Reliability — 15%

Recommendation Strength should remain editorial, not part of the raw composite.

Reason: implementation, playability, controls, and stability matter more than superficial excitement.

---

## 8. Recommendation Labels

Writers and editors should assign one of these public labels:

### Recommended
Use when:
- the VR route works well enough for a broad enthusiast audience
- the core experience meaningfully benefits from VR
- setup/stability caveats do not overwhelm the value

### Recommended with Caveats
Use when:
- the route is worthwhile, but clear tradeoffs exist
- not ideal for everyone
- setup, comfort, or performance issues need blunt disclosure

### Only for Enthusiasts / Tinkerers
Use when:
- the experience is real and interesting
- but the burden, instability, or weirdness is too high for most users

### Wait for Updates
Use when:
- the foundation is promising
- but current breakage or incompleteness makes recommendation premature

### Not Recommended
Use when:
- the current VR route is too broken, too compromised, too incomplete, or too painful to justify

---

## 9. How to Treat Different VR Routes Fairly

The site covers multiple types of VR access paths. Do not grade them by pretending they are interchangeable.

### Official hybrid support
Expectations should be highest for polish, support continuity, and accessibility.

### Official standalone VR version
Judge it as its own product, but compare honestly against the flat version where relevant.

### Full VR mod
Allow for some roughness, but do not hand-wave serious problems because it is "impressive for a mod."

### Framework-based VR
Be especially careful.
Framework compatibility can be fascinating, but many such experiences are curiosity-grade rather than recommendation-grade.

The question is still: should a reader actually do this?

---

## 10. Review Types

Different page types may use the same rubric with different depth.

### Full review
Use for high-value titles.
Requires meaningful direct testing and full scoring coverage.

### Compatibility / verdict page
Use for lower-tier titles.
Still requires verdict, classification, and evidence, but may be shorter.

### Setup guide
May support the review, but does not replace it.
A setup guide explains how; a review judges whether the result is worth doing.

---

## 11. Update Policy

A page should be reviewed or refreshed when:
- the game receives a major patch affecting compatibility
- the mod/framework receives meaningful updates
- control support changes substantially
- prior blockers are fixed
- the current recommendation is no longer accurate

Every page should show a **last verified date**.

This is both editorially useful and SEO-friendly.

---

## 12. Disclosure Standard

When relevant, disclose:
- whether access was free, purchased, provided, or reimbursed
- whether developers/modders had prior visibility into coverage
- whether affiliate links are used on the page

Trust compounds only if disclosure is normal and boring.

---

## 13. What Not to Do

Do not:
- review only the underlying game and pretend that covers the VR route
- over-credit novelty
- excuse major flaws because the idea is cool
- hide testing limitations
- inflate a framework experiment into a strong recommendation
- use community excitement as a substitute for evidence

---

## 14. Publication Threshold

A review is publishable only if:
- the VR route is correctly classified
- the verdict is clear
- testing is disclosed
- the page explains setup, playability, controls, comfort, performance, and stability
- known caveats are stated plainly
- the final recommendation matches the evidence

---

## 15. Editorial Bottom Line

The best review methodology in this category does one thing well:

> it helps readers make a better decision than they would have made from hype, store tags, Discord chatter, or a random YouTube video.

That is the standard.
