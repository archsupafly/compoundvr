## Lead

The Half-Life 2: VR Mod delivers one of the most faithful flat-to-VR conversions available—free to anyone who owns the original game—but its vehicle sections remain a stomach-churning test of VR tolerance despite two years of comfort refinements.

## Hardware & Setup

### What You Need

**Required:**
- Steam copy of Half-Life 2 (ownership verified, family sharing insufficient)
- SteamVR-compatible headset (Index, Quest via Link/Air Link, Vive, Windows MR, etc.)
- Windows 10
- 3–10 GB storage (varies by episode)

**Minimum Specs [Documentation]:**
- CPU: Intel i5-4590 / AMD Ryzen 1600
- GPU: NVIDIA GTX 1060 / AMD RX 580
- RAM: 8 GB

**Recommended Specs [Documentation]:**
- CPU: Intel 8th gen / AMD Ryzen 3000
- GPU: NVIDIA GTX 1080 / AMD RX 5700
- RAM: 16 GB

### Installation Walkthrough

Unlike many flat-to-VR mods that require manual file replacement, the Half-Life 2: VR Mod is **Steam-native**:

1. **Verify ownership:** Ensure Half-Life 2 is in your Steam library (purchased, not family-shared)
2. **Install the mod:** Search "Half-Life 2: VR Mod" on Steam, click Install
3. **Launch via Steam:** The mod appears as a separate library entry—launch from there, not from Half-Life 2
4. **SteamVR initializes:** The mod auto-detects your headset and controllers

**No launch arguments required.** No manual file placement. No external mod managers.

### First-Time Setup

Upon first launch, the mod prompts for:
- **Dominant hand:** Right or left (swaps all controls)
- **Locomotion:** Smooth (default) or teleport
- **Turning:** Snap (30° default) or smooth
- **Reloading:** Manual (Alyx-style) or quick reload

**Recommendation:** Start with smooth locomotion + snap turning. Teleport is available but limits combat fluidity.

### Controller Compatibility

**Tested/Documented:**
- Valve Index (full finger tracking support)
- Meta Quest 2/3/Pro (via SteamVR)
- HTC Vive wands
- Windows MR controllers

**Note [Community Report]:** Some users report binding conflicts with non-Index controllers. SteamVR controller binding customization is available if defaults feel awkward.

## Gameplay Experience

### The Core Conversion

The Source VR Mod Team didn't just add head tracking—they rebuilt Half-Life 2's interaction layer from scratch. The result feels closer to *Half-Life: Alyx* than a traditional port.

**What Works Exceptionally Well:**

**Weapon Handling [Documentation + Community Reports]:**
- **Radial weapon wheel:** Hold primary hand stick to summon a hexagonal grid of available weapons—position your hand over the desired weapon and release. Muscle memory develops quickly; experienced players can swap without looking.
- **Manual reloading:** Eject magazine with one button, grab fresh ammo from behind your shoulder, insert. Adds genuine tension to firefights.
- **Two-handed stabilization:** Rifles and the crossbow can be grabbed with the off-hand for steadier aim.
- **Iron sights:** Functional and properly aligned.

**Room-Scale Implementation [Documentation]:**
- Full 6DOF tracking with no artificial pushback
- Physical crouching works (and is encouraged)
- Leaning into walls triggers a gentle fade-to-black rather than collision rejection
- Object interaction: Grab, throw, and manipulate physics objects with either hand

**Sensory Highlights [Community Reports]:**
- **Scale:** City 17's architecture gains vertical presence—the Citadel looms overhead in ways the flat game never conveyed
- **Ravenholm:** The horror atmosphere translates effectively; zombies at life-size in dark corridors hit differently
- **Gravity Gun:** Manipulating physics objects by hand feels natural; the "catch" mechanic works with physical throws
- **Dog:** Playing catch with the robot actually involves physical tossing

### The Vehicle Problem

**Critical Comfort Issue:**

Half-Life 2's airboat and buggy sections were designed for flat screens. In VR, they remain the mod's biggest weakness despite mitigation efforts.

**What the Mod Does Right [Documentation]:**
- View smoothing filters out bumps and shakes
- Camera kept constantly upright (no roll)
- Decoupled camera option available
- Third-person camera option for extreme comfort needs

**The Reality [Community Reports]:**

Even with all comfort options enabled, vehicle sections cause motion sickness for a significant portion of players. The speed, rapid direction changes, and disconnect between visual motion and physical stillness create a vestibular conflict that filtering can't fully resolve.

**Recommendation:** If you're prone to motion sickness, use the third-person camera option for vehicle sections. It's less immersive but playable. Alternatively, take frequent breaks during Highway 17 and Riding Shotgun.

## Pros & Cons

### ✅ Pros

**1. Native Steam Integration**
One-click install, automatic updates, Steam Workshop support. No manual file management, no launcher juggling.

**2. Weapon Handling Excellence**
The radial menu and manual reloading feel derived from *Half-Life: Alyx*—intuitive, physical, and satisfying. Combat gains tactical depth when reloading under pressure requires actual dexterity.

**3. Full Trilogy Coverage**
Base game + both episodes, all free. The complete Half-Life 2 narrative is playable in VR.

**4. Accessibility Options**
Left-handed mode, teleport movement, laser sights, quick reload, and multiple comfort settings demonstrate thoughtful inclusive design.

**5. Room-Scale Done Right**
Physical movement matters. Leaning around corners, crouching behind cover, and physically grabbing objects transforms familiar encounters.

**6. Community Longevity**
2+ years post-release with ongoing patches, Workshop support, and active development. Not abandonware.

**7. Price**
Free with Half-Life 2 ownership. Exceptional value.

### ❌ Cons

**1. Vehicle Sections Remain Uncomfortable**
Despite comfort options, the airboat and buggy sequences are genuinely difficult for motion-sensitive players. No amount of smoothing fully resolves the fundamental disconnect.

**2. Visual Age**
2004 textures and geometry show their age in VR. The art direction holds up, but low-resolution assets are more noticeable at headset proximity. (Workshop HD packs help.)

**3. Missing Hand Animations**
Hands vanish when holding weapons/objects [Documentation: "planned, will be added gradually"]. Floating hands work fine but break presence momentarily.

**4. Learning Curve**
The weapon wheel and manual reloading require practice. Early combat can feel clumsy until muscle memory develops.

**5. SteamVR Lock-In**
No native Oculus/Meta runtime support. Quest users must run through SteamVR/Link, adding minor overhead.

**6. Ladder Climbing**
Immersive ladder climbing (grip-based) works but can be finicky. Stick-based climbing available as fallback.

## Verdict

### The Bottom Line

The Half-Life 2: VR Mod is a **confident recommendation** for anyone with:
- A SteamVR-compatible headset
- Half-Life 2 in their Steam library
- Moderate VR experience (or willingness to use teleport/comfort options)
- Tolerance for occasional vehicle-section discomfort

### Who Should Play This

**Definitely:**
- Half-Life 2 fans seeking a fresh perspective
- VR users who enjoyed *Half-Life: Alyx* and want more
- Players comfortable with smooth locomotion
- Anyone curious about flat-to-VR conversion quality

**Maybe Not:**
- First-time VR users (motion sickness risk)
- Players highly sensitive to vehicle movement
- Those expecting *Alyx*-level visual fidelity
