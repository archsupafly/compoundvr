# Elite Dangerous VR Brief

## Game Context
- **Title:** Elite Dangerous
- **Release:** 2014 (VR support added 2014 for DK2, 2015 for consumer headsets)
- **Developer:** Frontier Developments
- **Genre:** Space simulation, trading, combat, exploration
- **Core gameplay:** Piloting a spacecraft in a realistic 1:1 scale Milky Way galaxy, trading, bounty hunting, exploring, mining, passenger missions
- **Known for:** Massive scope (400 billion star systems), realistic physics, cockpit-centric design, long-term live service

## Route Type
**Official Hybrid** — Same game, VR mode toggle. Players switch between flat and VR in the same game/client.

## Platforms
- PCVR (SteamVR, Oculus SDK)
- Quest via Quest Link / Virtual Desktop (PCVR streaming)

## Research Focus
- How complete is the VR implementation? (cockpit vs external cameras)
- Motion controls vs HOTAS vs gamepad vs keyboard/mouse
- HUD and UI readability in VR
- Comfort options (vignette, snap turn, etc.)
- Performance requirements — this is a demanding game
- Can VR players play with flat players? (Yes — same universe)
- Engineering menu / FSS scanner usability in VR
- On-foot Odyssey content VR support
- Known issues or limitations

## Notes
- Elite Dangerous is often cited as one of the best VR implementations in gaming — the cockpit design makes it a natural fit
- The Horizon/Odyssey on-foot content had VR issues at launch — need current status
- HOTAS (Hands On Throttle And Stick) is the recommended input method in VR — worth noting
- The game is overwhelming for new players — VR adds another layer of complexity
- Long-term live service — any VR issues should be checked for current status (2025-2026)
