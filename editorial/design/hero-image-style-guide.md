# CompoundVR Hero Image Style Guide

## Brand Context
**Direction:** "Technical Authority" — sophisticated, editorial, light body/dark header  
**References:** Wired, Engadget, Nexus Mods  
**Tone:** Credible, premium, not hype-driven

---

## Research Synthesis

### VR-Specific Publications

#### UploadVR Style
- **Approach:** Immersive in-headset photography, first-person perspective shots, hardware integration
- **Treatment:** Clean, modern, product-aware. Often shows headsets as part of the experience
- **Composition:** Dynamic angles showing VR action, motion controllers visible, sense of presence
- **Mood:** "This is happening now" — immediacy, current relevance
- **Key Visual:** VR headset + hands/controllers + game environment blend
- **Color:** Neutral to slightly warm, high clarity

#### Road to VR Style
- **Approach:** Editorial depth, documentary-style, technical credibility
- **Treatment:** More conservative than UploadVR, less flashy, more information-dense
- **Composition:** Straightforward, clear focal points, often hardware-forward
- **Mood:** Trustworthy authority, "we've been covering this since the beginning"
- **Key Visual:** Clean product shots, side profiles, technical detail visible
- **Color:** Muted, professional, sometimes slightly desaturated

### Gaming Media

#### IGN Style
- **Approach:** High production values, cinematic, entertainment-first
- **Treatment:** Polished game screenshots, dramatic lighting, action-oriented
- **Composition:** Wide cinematic shots, rule of thirds, dynamic angles
- **Mood:** Epic, exciting, "event" coverage
- **Key Visual:** Heroic moments, explosions, character-focused
- **Color:** Vibrant but controlled, film-like grading
- **Note for CompoundVR:** Too hype-driven; borrow production values but not tone

#### Push Square Style
- **Approach:** Community-focused, platform-specific, enthusiast credibility
- **Treatment:** Clean, accessible, hardware-aware
- **Composition:** Product shots mixed with gameplay, clear hierarchy
- **Mood:** "Your gaming buddy who knows their stuff"
- **Key Visual:** PlayStation-centric (for them), platform pride
- **Color:** Bright, welcoming, slightly saturated

#### Eurogamer Style
- **Approach:** Technical depth, PC gaming focus, informed analysis
- **Treatment:** Performance graphs mixed with screenshots, detail-oriented
- **Composition:** Information-rich, screenshot + data overlays
- **Mood:** "We measured this"
- **Key Visual:** Clean screenshots with technical annotations
- **Color:** Neutral, functional

#### Rock Paper Shotgun Style
- **Approach:** Personality-driven, indie-friendly, distinctive voice
- **Treatment:** Less polished, more authentic, "from the community"
- **Composition:** Often unconventional, humorous, or striking
- **Mood:** Smart, slightly irreverent, trustworthy
- **Key Visual:** Whatever serves the story — flexibility over consistency
- **Color:** Varied, adaptable to content

#### PC Gamer Style
- **Approach:** Hardware authority, optimization focus, practical
- **Treatment:** Clean product shots, benchmark awareness
- **Composition:** System specs visible, FPS counters welcome
- **Mood:** "Will it run on your rig?"
- **Key Visual:** Performance meets aesthetics
- **Color:** Tech-forward, RGB-aware but not dominant

#### Kotaku Style
- **Approach:** Personality-driven, culturally aware, occasionally controversial
- **Treatment:** Busy, multi-element, collage-like
- **Composition:** Multiple images, text overlays, dynamic layouts
- **Mood:** Conversation starter
- **Key Visual:** Attention-grabbing, sometimes chaotic
- **Note for CompoundVR:** Avoid — too busy for our authority positioning

### Tech/Enthusiast Media

#### Wired Editorial Style
- **Approach:** Cinematic photography, documentary-style, high production value
- **Treatment:** Desaturated color grading, subtle contrast, moody but not dark
- **Composition:** Often center-weighted subjects, negative space for text overlay
- **Mood:** Serious, investigative, "this matters"
- **Aspect:** Wide horizontal (16:9 to 21:9) with safe zones for mobile cropping
- **Key Visual:** Like a magazine cover spread
- **Color:** Film-like, lifted blacks, sophisticated palette

#### The Verge Style
- **Approach:** Design-forward, modern aesthetic, intentional motion
- **Treatment:** Clean lines, geometric awareness, video-forward
- **Composition:** Bold, graphic, sometimes abstract
- **Mood:** "The future, well-designed"
- **Key Visual:** Product as sculpture, environmental context
- **Color:** Controlled, often monochromatic with accent colors

#### Engadget Tech Journalism Style
- **Approach:** Clean product shots, lifestyle integration, sharp focus
- **Treatment:** Bright but not blown out, tech-forward aesthetic
- **Composition:** Product-forward, minimal distractions, centered subject
- **Mood:** Accessible authority, "we tested this"
- **Aspect:** Wide banners with clear focal points
- **Color:** Clean whites, subtle gradients, professional

#### Ars Technica Style
- **Approach:** Technical depth, knowledge-assumed, no fluff
- **Treatment:** Screenshot-forward, UI visible, evidence-based
- **Composition:** Information density over aesthetics
- **Mood:** "You either know or you don't"
- **Key Visual:** Functional over beautiful
- **Color:** Neutral, utilitarian

### Modding Community

#### Nexus Mods Style
- **Approach:** Game screenshots with polish, community-focused imagery
- **Treatment:** In-game capture with post-processing, UI-aware
- **Composition:** Action-oriented, immersive perspective
- **Mood:** Enthusiast-credible, "this actually works"
- **Aspect:** Responsive-friendly, works at multiple crop ratios
- **Key Visual:** "Before/after" potential, transformation implied

### Retro/Nostalgia

#### Retro Dodo Style
- **Approach:** Nostalgia-driven, modern presentation of classic content
- **Treatment:** Vibrant colors, celebration of pixel art/retro aesthetics
- **Composition:** Centered, celebratory, product-forward
- **Mood:** Joyful remembering, modern retro
- **Key Visual:** Classic hardware/games presented beautifully
- **Color:** Saturated, warm, inviting

## Key Insight: VR vs Gaming vs Tech Hero Images

### How VR-Specific Sites Differ:

**VR-Specific (UploadVR, Road to VR):**
- Show the headset OR imply first-person perspective
- Hardware is part of the story
- Emphasis on "presence" and immersion
- Motion controllers often visible
- Technical credibility (tracking, FOV, etc.)

**General Gaming (IGN, Eurogamer, PC Gamer):**
- Focus on game content, not hardware
- Cinematic moments, character-focused
- Less concern with "how it feels in VR"
- Traditional screenshot approach
- Performance and playability focus

**Tech Media (Wired, The Verge, Engadget):**
- Product as object of study
- Design-forward, aesthetic appreciation
- Context and environment matter
- "Technology as lifestyle"
- Sophisticated visual treatment

### The Right Blend for CompoundVR:

**Editorial First (Wired/Engadget influence):**
- Cinematic, documentary-style photography
- Desaturated, film-like color grading
- Negative space for text overlay
- Sophisticated, not hype-driven

**Gaming Authority (Eurogamer/PC Gamer influence):**
- In-game authenticity
- Performance awareness
- Technical credibility
- Screenshot-forward but polished

**VR-Specific Context (UploadVR/Road to VR influence):**
- Implied immersion (first-person perspective)
- Hardware awareness without hardware dominance
- "Presence" without gimmicks
- Community credibility signals

**Modding Authenticity (Nexus Mods influence):**
- Real gameplay, not marketing renders
- "This actually works" visual proof
- Before/after transformation subtlety
- Functional beauty

## CompoundVR Hero Image Standards (Updated v2.0)

### Core Principles

1. **Editorial First, Gaming Second**
   - Avoid "gaming blog" tropes (explosions, over-saturated neons, busy compositions)
   - Think magazine cover, not YouTube thumbnail
   - Subdued confidence over loud excitement

2. **Cinematic Documentation**
   - Images should feel like captured moments, not staged marketing
   - Prefer in-game perspectives that show the experience authentically
   - Post-processing should enhance, not oversell

3. **Technical Authority Visual Cues**
   - Sharp focus where it matters
   - Controlled depth of field when appropriate
   - Clean lines, intentional composition

4. **VR Without VR Gimmicks**
   - Show immersion through perspective, not literal headset overlay
   - First-person framing implies VR presence
   - Avoid cheesy "person wearing headset" stock photos

### Image Treatment Specifications

| Element | Specification |
|---------|---------------|
| **Color Palette** | Desaturated, cinematic tones. Avoid pure blacks. Prefer muted teals, warm ambers, neutral grays |
| **Contrast** | Medium-high, but with lifted blacks. Film-like, not crushed |
| **Saturation** | Reduced 15-25% from native. Let color breathe, not scream |
| **Vignette** | Subtle if used (5-10%). Center-weight focus without heavy borders |
| **Sharpening** | Moderate. Detail visible, not oversharpened |
| **Grain** | Optional subtle film grain (2-4%) for editorial texture |

### Aspect Ratio & Cropping

| Use Case | Ratio | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|
| **Desktop Hero** | 21:9 (2520×1080) | Full cinematic width |
| **Desktop Fallback** | 16:9 (1920×1080) | Standard widescreen |
| **Mobile Hero** | 16:9 or 3:2 | Center-weighted safe zone |
| **Mobile Cropped** | 1:1 or 4:3 | Critical content in center 60% |

**Safe Zone Rule:** Keep critical visual elements within the center 60% horizontally and 70% vertically. Edges may be cropped on mobile.

### Composition Guidelines

**DO:**
- Center-weight important subjects
- Use rule of thirds for horizon lines
- Include negative space for potential text overlay (top 30% often clear)
- Show authentic gameplay moments
- Prefer atmospheric/moody over action-packed
- Consider VR-specific perspectives (first-person, lens-aware framing)
- Think "editorial photography" not "marketing asset"

**DON'T:**
- Overload with UI elements, HUD, or text
- Use pure black backgrounds (aim for #1a1a1a or lighter)
- Create busy compositions that fight with headline text
- Over-saturate or use "gamer RGB" aesthetics
- Include watermarks, logos, or border graphics
- Show literal headset-on-head unless essential

### Image Source Hierarchy

1. **In-Game Photography Mode** — Ideal. Native resolution, controlled camera
2. **Gameplay Capture** — Acceptable with post-processing
3. **Composite/Illustration** — Use sparingly, must feel authentic
4. **Stock Photography** — Avoid unless heavily treated to match style

### The "Flat to VR" Visual Language

When showing flat-to-VR transformation:
- **Subtle approach:** Single compelling VR perspective (trust the reader to understand)
- **Comparative approach:** Split treatment with clear visual separation, not jarring juxtaposition
- **Abstract approach:** Visual metaphor (lens distortion, depth layers, perspective shift)

Avoid literal "before/after" sliders in static imagery. The editorial style should imply sophistication.

### Category-Specific Treatments

| Content Type | Visual Approach | Primary Influence |
|--------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Reviews** | Cinematic screenshot, atmospheric, performance-implied | Wired + Eurogamer |
| **Guides/How-To** | Clean, instructional, clarity-first | Engadget + Nexus Mods |
| **News** | Current, relevant, immediate | UploadVR + The Verge |
| **Retrospective** | Nostalgic but polished, modern treatment | Retro Dodo + Wired |
| **Technical Analysis** | Detail-rich, screenshot + data | Ars Technica + PC Gamer |
| **Community** | Authentic, user-generated feel | Nexus Mods + RPS |

## Quality Checklist

Before approving a hero image:

- [ ] Color treatment matches cinematic/desaturated spec
- [ ] No text or watermarks visible
- [ ] Mobile crop safe (center 60% holds the image)
- [ ] Works against light body background (#fafafa)
- [ ] Feels premium, not hype-driven
- [ ] Tells a story or sets a mood without explanation
- [ ] Would fit on Wired or Engadget without modification
- [ ] Implies VR without cliché headset photos
- [ ] Shows authentic gameplay, not marketing renders
- [ ] Maintains technical credibility signals
